Sotho Foods Sell Traditional Food and include:

Nyekoe

To prepare Nyekoe, beans are soaked overnight to soften them before being cooked with sorghum, a cereal grain that adds a unique texture and nutty flavor. As the beans and sorghum cook together, they create a thick and flavorful base for the stew. Small pieces of pumpkin are then added to the pot, introducing a natural sweetness and a vibrant color.

Seketsa

Seketsa is a delightfully fluffy and energy-packed dish made with mashed pumpkin or squash, maize meal, milk, cream, butter, salt, and white pepper. It's a popular and beloved dish throughout Lesotho. Here's an image of Seketsa: The natural sweetness of the pumpkin complements the creaminess of the pap, creating a delicious and satisfying meal. Seketsa is often served as a breakfast or side dish, and it can also be enjoyed on its own.

Potele

Basotho consider Potele traditional food as a significant dish in their cuisine. Potele is a traditional Basotho dish that holds cultural importance and is part of the Basotho culinary heritage. It is a dish that reflects the traditional food culture of the Basotho people, showcasing their culinary traditions and practices
